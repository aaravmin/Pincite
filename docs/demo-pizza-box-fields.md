## Step 2 - Disclosure (Invention intake)

Fill each field in the left nav, then open All fields to review and save.

- Technical field / industry:
  Food packaging and food transport containers, specifically single-serve hot-food containers such as pizza boxes.

- Problem solved:
  Standard square pizza boxes trap steam between the pizza and the flat bottom, so the crust turns soggy before it is served.
  The square footprint also wastes material and space around a round pizza.
  Multi-piece boxes add cost and require assembly.

- How it works:
  A round container is formed as a single preformed piece, with the lid joined to the base by an integral hinge, so no assembly is needed.
  Concentric ridges on the base floor lift the pizza off the bottom so steam is not trapped under it.
  Openings through the base floor give that steam a path out of the container.
  A moisture-channeling feature formed into the underside of the lid collects condensation from above and routes it to the perimeter instead of dripping back onto the food.
  A latch holds the container closed in transit.

- Key components:
  base portion 110
  base floor 112
  concentric ridges 114
  openings 116
  base sidewall 118
  lid portion 120
  lid top wall 122
  moisture-channeling feature 124
  lid sidewall 126
  mating rim 128
  hinged connection 130
  latch 140
  bezel ring 150

- Advantages:
  Keeps the crust crisp by removing steam from under and above the food.
  Single-piece preformed construction is cost effective and needs no assembly.
  Uses less material than a square box for a round pizza and can be molded from recycled fiber.
  Round, low-profile shape stacks and ships compactly.

- Alternatives and variations:
  The number of openings can vary (for example four, six, or eight).
  The ridges can be concentric rings, a spiral, or a radial pattern of standoffs.
  The container can be molded fiber, paperboard, or a compostable polymer.
  The latch can be a snap-fit rim feature or a separate tab.

### Background
Hot food such as pizza is commonly transported in square corrugated boxes.
A pizza placed on the flat bottom of such a box rests directly on the surface, so steam released by the food is trapped between the food and the bottom.
The trapped steam condenses and is reabsorbed by the crust, which becomes soft and soggy before the food is served.
Square boxes also enclose a round food item inefficiently, wasting material at the corners and taking more shelf and transport space than the food requires.
Many containers are also assembled from multiple pieces, which adds cost and labor.
A need remains for a low-cost, single-piece container that actively removes moisture from above and below a round hot-food item.

### Brief summary
A container 100 for transporting hot food is disclosed.
The container 100 is singularly constructed from a single piece of material in a preformed manner that requires no assembly before use.
The container 100 includes a base portion 110 having a base floor 112 and a base sidewall 118, and a lid portion 120 having a lid top wall 122 and a lid sidewall 126, the lid portion 120 coupled to the base portion 110 by a hinged connection 130.
Concentric ridges 114 on the base floor 112 elevate a food item 200 above the base floor 112 so that steam is not trapped beneath the food item 200.
Openings 116 through the base floor 112 expel the steam from the container 100.
A moisture-channeling feature 124 in the underside of the lid portion 120 collects condensation and directs it toward the perimeter rather than onto the food item 200.
A latch 140 retains the lid portion 120 in a closed position.

### Detailed description
Referring to FIG. 1 and FIG. 5, a container 100 for transporting hot food is singularly constructed from a single piece of material in a preformed manner, so that no assembly of the container 100 is required before use.
The container 100 includes a base portion 110 and a lid portion 120 coupled to the base portion 110 by a hinged connection 130, the base portion 110, the lid portion 120, and the hinged connection 130 being formed from the single piece of material.
The container 100 is circular in plan and sized to receive a round food item 200, such as a personal pizza approximately 14 inches in diameter, with a bezel ring 150 of approximately 0.5 inch surrounding the food item 200 to locate it and limit movement during transport.
In one embodiment, the container 100 is molded from recycled fiber, although paperboard or a compostable polymer may be used.

Referring to FIG. 3 and FIG. 6, the base portion 110 includes a base floor 112 and a base sidewall 118 extending upward from a perimeter of the base floor 112.
A plurality of concentric ridges 114 projects upward from the base floor 112.
The concentric ridges 114 provide structural support for the container 100 and contact and elevate the food item 200 above the base floor 112, defining a gap between the food item 200 and the base floor 112.
Because the food item 200 is held above the base floor 112, steam released by the food item 200 is not trapped against the underside of the food item 200.
A plurality of openings 116 extends through the base floor 112 and provides a path by which steam in the gap is expelled from the container 100, so that moisture does not condense beneath the food item 200 and is not reabsorbed by the food item 200.

Referring to FIG. 2 and FIG. 6, the lid portion 120 includes a lid top wall 122 and a lid sidewall 126 depending from a perimeter of the lid top wall 122.
A moisture-channeling feature 124 is integrally formed in an underside of the lid top wall 122.
Steam that rises within the container 100 condenses on the underside of the lid portion 120, and the moisture-channeling feature 124 collects the condensation and directs it toward the lid sidewall 126 and the perimeter of the container 100, rather than allowing it to fall back onto the food item 200.

Referring to FIG. 4, the lid portion 120 pivots about the hinged connection 130 between an open position and a closed position.
A latch 140 at a side of the container 100 opposite the hinged connection 130 retains the lid portion 120 in the closed position and resists accidental opening during transport.
A mating rim 128 of the lid portion 120 seats against a corresponding rim of the base portion 110 when the container 100 is closed.

In operation, a hot food item 200 is placed on the concentric ridges 114 and the lid portion 120 is closed about the hinged connection 130 and secured by the latch 140.
During transport, steam from the food item 200 escapes downward through the openings 116 and is channeled away from above by the moisture-channeling feature 124, so the food item 200 remains crisp until the container 100 is opened.

### Claims
1. A container for transporting hot food, the container comprising: a base portion having a base floor and a sidewall extending from the base floor; a plurality of ridges projecting from the base floor and configured to elevate a food item above the base floor; a plurality of openings extending through the base floor and configured to expel steam from the container; and a lid portion coupled to the base portion by a hinged connection, the lid portion having a moisture-channeling feature formed in an underside thereof, wherein the base portion, the lid portion, and the hinged connection are singularly constructed from a single piece of material in a preformed manner that requires no assembly before use.
2. The container of claim 1, wherein the base portion and the lid portion are circular.
3. The container of claim 1, wherein the plurality of ridges comprises a plurality of concentric ridges.
4. The container of claim 1, wherein the plurality of openings consists of eight openings arranged around the base floor.
5. The container of claim 1, further comprising a latch positioned opposite the hinged connection and configured to retain the lid portion in a closed position.
6. The container of claim 1, wherein the single piece of material is molded recycled fiber.
7. The container of claim 1, wherein the base portion and the lid portion define an interior sized to receive a round food item with a bezel of about 0.5 inch surrounding the food item.
8. A method of transporting hot food, the method comprising: placing a food item on a plurality of ridges that elevate the food item above a base floor of a container that is singularly constructed from a single piece of material; closing a lid portion onto a base portion about an integral hinged connection; expelling steam from beneath the food item through a plurality of openings in the base floor; and channeling condensation away from the food item with a moisture-channeling feature formed in an underside of the lid portion.

### Abstract
A container transports hot food while keeping it crisp.
The container is singularly constructed from a single piece of material in a preformed manner that requires no assembly, with a lid portion joined to a base portion by an integral hinged connection.
Concentric ridges on the base floor elevate the food above the floor, and openings through the floor expel steam from beneath the food.
A moisture-channeling feature on the underside of the lid collects condensation and directs it to the perimeter instead of onto the food.
A latch holds the lid closed during transport, and the single piece can be molded from recycled fiber.
